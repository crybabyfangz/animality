The Bear

Inner: The system registers an immediate threat of overwhelming internal emotional pressure that threatens to destroy everything, making survival entirely dependent on absorbing and containing the stress entirely so it cannot damage the surroundings.

Outer: This internal focus quietly internalizes immense pain, tension, and pressure, entirely ignoring their own need for release to maintain a calm exterior. However, when the pressure reaches maximum capacity, the internal containment suffers a sudden, catastrophic failure, resulting in an uncontained, highly volatile explosion of raw emotion. Because the long building phase happens entirely in secret, observers routinely mistake this explosive eruption for completely unprovoked, irrational impulsivity.

Influence: When carrying this influence, the manifest behavioral defense takes on a distinct pressure-cooker dynamic. The primary defense mechanism will heavily compress and bottle its outward reactions initially, introducing a silent, mounting tension to the execution that threatens a massive, volatile eruption if pushed past its limit.
