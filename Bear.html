BEAR

The bear response emerges when emotional or situational pressure exceeds the system’s ability to contain it internally for extended periods. Instability is not processed gradually; it accumulates until containment fails and expression occurs rapidly.

Perception continues functioning during accumulation, but emotional load builds beneath the surface without immediate resolution. The system operates normally until internal threshold is reached.

Under stress, release becomes sudden and intense rather than gradual. The system transitions quickly from containment to overflow once internal limits are exceeded.

In social environments, this may appear as controlled presence followed by sudden emotional or behavioral intensity when pressure can no longer be contained.

In environmental contexts, instability is absorbed until capacity is exceeded, at which point response becomes strong and immediate rather than measured.

At its most extreme expression, this becomes rapid emotional overflow triggered by accumulation rather than immediate cause.

Internal Presentation

Internally, this state accumulates emotional or situational pressure without immediate release, maintaining function until threshold collapse occurs.

External Presentation

Externally, this appears as endurance under load followed by sudden and intense reaction once internal capacity is exceeded.

Influence Layer

Influence manifests as delayed pressure release. Effects on environment occur not immediately, but in concentrated form after accumulation.
