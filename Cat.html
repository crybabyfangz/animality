The Cat

Inner: The system registers an immediate threat of dangerous emotional intrusion, making survival entirely dependent on achieving absolute autonomy, self-containment, and isolation from external influence.

Outer: This internal focus triggers a sudden, total internal shutdown of all social channels. They withdraw completely into their own private orbit, cutting off emotional transmission to track and stabilize their own internal state in a vacuum. Because they completely isolate themselves from the emotional frequency of the room, their outer defense appears to others as entirely cold, unbothered, and aggressively self-reliant, often leaving a trail of deep interpersonal alienation.

Influence: As an underlying influence, it adds an icy layer of emotional detachment to the outward execution of the primary defense mechanism. Regardless of the internal state or the core animal's needs, it filters the active behavioral expression, forcing the manifest defense to appear completely closed-off, independent, and unbothered by the chaos of the outside world.
