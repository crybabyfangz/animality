The Dog

Inner: The system registers an immediate threat of isolation and abandonment. Survival becomes entirely dependent on securing immediate physical proximity, relational familiarity, and historical comfort, driven by a deep craving for genuine closeness and relational safety.

Outer: This internal focus automatically dissolves personal boundaries, causing them to melt into the people around them. They adopt postures of immediate submission, create intense social distractions, or seek intense proximity to guarantee they are not left alone to face the danger. They will execute any relational loop necessary to force the environment back into a stable state of mutual connection, a survival reflex that observers heavily misunderstand as deep, toxic codependency.

Influence: As an underlying influence, it softens the primary behavior with an urgent undercurrent to people-please or seek safety in numbers. Even if the primary defense is cold, calculated, or aggressive, the execution is forced to lean heavily on familiar relational anchors, past comforting habits, or desperate attempts to keep the surrounding group appeased and connected.
