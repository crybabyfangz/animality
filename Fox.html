The Fox

Inner: The system registers an immediate threat of exposure or direct confrontation, triggering an absolute internal requirement for evasion, frictionlessness, and a total escape from accountability.

Outer: This vulnerability forces a fluid, real-time rewriting of facts, timelines, and narratives. The nervous system alters the presentation of reality on the fly to slide through the cracks of a threat before a connection can be pinned down. Because their version of events shifts instantly to keep them shielded, others often perceive them as inconsistent, untrustworthy, or compulsive liars.

Influence: The primary behavior is glazed with a layer of natural narrative warping. Regardless of how the primary defense operates, the individual will instinctively bend the reported sequence of events, unconsciously distorting the timeline to align perfectly with their immediate perception and slide away from consequence.
