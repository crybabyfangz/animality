The Rabbit

Inner: The system registers an immediate threat that demands immediate, total absence, rendering any form of remaining in the line of fire an unacceptable hazard to survival.

Outer: This internal focus executes an instantaneous, thoughtless escape sequence, fleeing the physical space or completely dropping the interaction at the very first available window. There is no negotiation, no standing ground, and no pausing to calculate a counter-strategy; the nervous system demands immediate absence. Because they prioritize a swift, silent exit over any form of engagement, their defensive flight is almost universally misinterpreted by others as sheer cowardice.

Influence: Functioning as an influence gives the primary behavior a distinct, unmistakable twitchiness and a highly sensitive flight threshold. Even if the individual is actively locked in a completely different defense mechanism—such as fighting back or trying to control the room—the second an exit opens up, the primary behavior will instantly drop away in favor of a sudden, swift exit.
