The Wolf

Inner: The system registers an immediate threat of chaos, triggering an absolute internal demand for structural equilibrium and predictability.

Outer: Panic translates directly into an automated, micro-managerial choreography. The immediate reflex forces them to sort, regiment, and control variables within their immediate orbit, extending even to the utter possession and tracking of social networks or actively managing other people's relationships to keep the environment from fracturing. Because this defense relies on rewriting rules, monitoring alliances, and organizing the people around them, observers frequently misinterpret the reaction as intentional possessiveness, cold rigidity, or a hostile power grab.

Influence: The behavior infuses the primary defense with a persistent need for structural grounding. While protecting themselves, the individual will simultaneously attempt to re-stabilize the physical layout, rewrite the unspoken rules, or shift the interpersonal boundaries of the situation to regain a sense of footing.
